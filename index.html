Meaning

Pacu

Competition; Race.

From the verb to accelerate: to speed up or make run fast/quickly.

Jalur

Longboat; Sampan.

A local term in Kuantan Singingi for a large boat made from a single, solid log.

Jalur Parts

Haluan

The boat's head

The foremost tip of the boat, which serves as a post for the 'Tukang Tari' (the dancer/cue-giver), functions as a water-splitter (like common boats do).

Roles in
this part ← Scroll

Tukang Tari

(Rhythm Keeper / Cue-Giver)

The individual standing at the bow, responsible for setting the paddling rhythm and boosting morale (popularly known as 'Aura Farming'). Their actions are crucial for crew synchronization.

Jalang

(Lifter/Balancer)

A small group responsible for balancing the boat and providing visual signals to the Tukang Tari up front.

Badan

Jalur's body

The main hull of the boat, where the driving force is generated. It is uniquely made from a single, jointless tree trunk, often reaching 25–35 meters in length.

this part ← Scroll

Tukang Concang

(Paddler)

They are the largest group of crew members (40 to 60 people) responsible for paddling. Their unity and paddling rhythm are crucial for winning.

Buritan

Jalur's tail

decorated with carvings or flags as an identity marker. It houses the main rudder for the Tukang Mudi's (Helmsman) directional control, reflecting the Jalur's unique aesthetic and philosophy.

Tukang Pinggang

The helmsman who steers the boat using the large paddle, making sure the Jalur maintains its course and avoids opponents.

Tukang Onjai

(Back Balancer)

The person responsible for balancing the boat and providing visual signals to the Tukang Tari at the bow.
